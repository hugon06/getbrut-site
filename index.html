FEATURES

The canvas, shown working.

A node canvas at decision altitude.

Prompt, refine, generate, compose, inpaint, review — one graph. Nodes are shots and decisions, not samplers and latents. Wires carry images, prompts and plans; every node shows its result inline.

19 node types · image + video + text · drag-to-connect · Tab palette

Multi-Shot: one scene, twelve opinions.

A planner writes N distinct shot prompts from your base images, then fills a contact sheet in parallel. Re-roll one tile for cents. Curate with ✓/✗ — only approved shots flow downstream.

per-tile re-roll · per-tile cache · verdicts flow to the next node
the spend ledger — every cent, itemized by provider · screenshot lands with the beta build

The cache is a feature with a euro sign.

Every node hashes its inputs. Unchanged inputs = cached output = nothing billed. A revision round touches three shots? You pay for three shots. The ledger shows every cent and who received it.

input-hash caching · per-node history · re-pin any past result

Video with a confirm gate, not a surprise bill.

Video is where budgets die. Brut quotes every clip — model, duration, resolution, estimated cost — and generates nothing until you confirm the total. Cancel a line, the total updates.

data-driven price estimates · itemized confirm before any spend

Inpaint, compose, conform — finish without round-trips.

Paint a mask, type what changes, only that region changes — with the rest pixel-faithful. Compose scenes from references. Conform aspect ratios locally for free.

native masks (OpenAI) · guided masks (Gemini) · local crop/conform, €0.00

Projects that live where your work lives.

First-class projects with their own outputs, graphs and spend ledger — on your disk or your NAS, adoptable by a second seat. Export a whole project as one .brutpack file, warm caches included. Reusable composites and templates travel in a global library.

FAQ

Six questions, blunt answers.

Where do my API keys live?+

In your OS keychain, on your machine. They are never logged, never sent to us, never written into a graph file. We couldn't leak them; we don't have them.

So what will I eventually pay for?+

The tool: the canvas, the caching that never re-bills unchanged work, the ledgers, the catalog kept current as providers churn. Never for tokens.

Is this a ComfyUI alternative?+

Different altitude. ComfyUI orchestrates model internals; Brut orchestrates creative decisions and the money attached. Their nodes are samplers; ours are shots.

What do I need to run it?+

Windows (macOS later), Python bootstraps itself via the launcher, and your own provider keys with billing enabled. Note: most providers' free tiers have zero image quota — a first-call 429 means billing isn't enabled, not that you're rate-limited.

My client work is under NDA.+

Good, ours too. Local-first means renders never leave your machine; there is no cloud to leak from. That's a compliance feature, not a slogan.

What happens if I stop paying (after launch)?+

After 12 paid months you keep a perpetual license to your version, forever. A lapsed Brut keeps working; paying resumes updates. Nobody can turn your tool off remotely — that would betray the whole thesis.
