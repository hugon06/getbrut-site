Video nodes

Where stills become motion. Brut treats video like the expensive thing it is — every clip is quoted and confirmed before a cent is spent.

#Image → Video

Animate a single image into a clip. Wire an image into the node, pick a provider and model, set duration/resolution and (where supported) audio and an end frame, and run.

Providers: Kling (REST and subscription), Google Veo, and Seedance — see Providers. Some models do text-to-video too. The node shows a cost estimate before you run, derived from Brut's data-driven pricing.

#Film Shots — the cinema batch executor

Film Shots takes a shot plan (from the DoP / Storyboard) and shoots the whole sequence. Its defining feature is the cost guard:

  • It prices every clip — model, duration, resolution, estimated cost — and shows an itemized confirmation before any spend. Cancel a line and the total updates.
  • Cached and pinned clips cost $0 and are never re-quoted.
  • It fans out on a worker pool (staggered to respect provider rate limits), with the same per-clip semantics as Multi-Shot: cache / ↻ re-roll / ✓✗ verdicts / 📌 pins.
  • An anti-shake stability clause is appended to every clip, and audio is an explicit AND-gate.

Nothing is generated until you confirm the total. That confirm no longer freezes the rest of a running graph — other branches keep working while it waits.

#Clip Board

The review board for Film Shots — playing video tiles, ✓ / ✗ / ↻ per clip, and per-tile clip-<i> plugs to route one clip onward. The video equivalent of the Shot Board.

#Sequence — local assembly, free

Wire your curated clips into a Sequence node and it assembles them into one mp4 with local ffmpeg — cuts or chained crossfades, scaled and padded to a common size. It's free, cached, and needs no ledger.

  • ffmpeg ships as a static binary inside Brut (no system install).
  • v1 is video-only (clip audio is dropped on assembly — audio-aware concat is the first follow-up).

#The full flow

The video nodes are the back half of the Cinema Sessions: Storyboard → Film Shots → Clip Board → Sequence → Preview. Read that walkthrough to see them working together.
