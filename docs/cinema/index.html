The Cinema Sessions

Brut's most ambitious workflow: turn a handful of stills into a finished, cut sequence — planned by a crew of AI agents, shot on the video provider of your choice, and assembled locally with ffmpeg, for exactly the bill it showed you.

Stills → a crew that plans the film → a storyboard you edit → clips shot behind a confirm gate → a sequence cut on your machine. You stay in control at every step.

#The pipeline

Conform → Reference Board → Scenarist → Artistic Director → DoP → Script Supervisor
        → Storyboard → Film Shots → Clip Board → Sequence → Preview

Every arrow is a wire. The agents pass a plan document (JSON on text wires); the boards are where you review and curate. Let's walk it.

#1 · Prepare the stills

Start from your renders — an Image In node (or a Multi-Shot contact sheet you've curated). Run them through Conform to a single aspect ratio (video wants consistency), cropping each to keep the right part of the frame.

Wire the conformed stills into a Reference Board. This makes the pool visible and numbered — and the tile number is the still number the crew will refer to. Reorder to taste; the numbers are the contract.

#2 · The crew plans the film

Chain the four agents, each grounded on the stills:

  • Scenarist writes the treatment (one shot = one still).
  • Artistic Director sets the look — palette, light, mood.
  • DoP turns it into an executable shot plan: camera, motion, duration, and which still grounds each clip.
  • Script Supervisor harmonizes speed, light and palette across the whole thing.

Pick the LLM for each — Gemini, GPT or Claude — on your own key. Each plan is editable text: tweak a line and it flows downstream; the analysis shows in the Activity panel.

#3 · The storyboard — your edit bay

The Storyboard turns the plan into shot cards: per-clip start/end frame swaps with thumbnails, editable and lockable prompts, add/reorder/remove shots. Change a line here and only that shot re-renders. It emits concrete image paths, so the next node just needs the plan wire.

#4 · Shoot — behind the confirm gate

Wire the Storyboard into Film Shots. It prices every clip — model, duration, resolution, cost — and shows an itemized confirmation. Nothing generates until you approve the total; cancel a line and it updates. Cached and pinned clips cost $0. Then it fans out, staggered to respect rate limits, with per-clip re-roll / ✓✗ / pin.

#5 · Review & cut

The Clip Board plays every clip — keep the good, ✗ the misses, ↻ re-roll one. Route the keepers into a Sequence node, which assembles them into one mp4 with local ffmpeg — cuts or crossfades, free. Drop a Preview on the end.

#Make it reusable

Select the four agents and collapse them into a "Cinema Team" composite — one droppable node with the stills as its inlets and the plan as its outlet. Save it to the library and start every film from it.

Why it holds together: grounding is enforced in code, not prose — one shot to one still, the DoP's still indices reconciled automatically, an anti-shake stability clause on every clip. The plan you approve is the film you get.
